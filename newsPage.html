Thanks for the memories; a tribute to the graduating seniors of 2013

  • by Greg Clementi Sports Editor gpclementi@yahoo.com
  • Jun 12, 2013

Student/athletes will be remembered for effort and character

The graduating class of male student/athletes of 2013 from Analy and El Molino High Schools will be remembered for their many accomplishments both on the field and in the classroom.

The athletes selected in this issue were among the best and brightest of their class and all had one thing in common: They represented their school and teams with tremendous talent, dedication and sportsmanship.

Although there were too many seniors to list, we congratulate each of them for their accomplishments and pay tribute to a few with words and pictures. The following is part two of a two-part series.

(1) ATHLETICISM Analys Darin Newman had few peers in a star-studded reign among the Tigers best all-around athletes in football, basketball and baseball. As a prolific passer, he put together one of the best grid seasons ever in leading the team to the SCL title and NCS playoffs. He was also a key man off the bench as a hoops guard in helping Analy to a league championship. In baseball, Newman was an MVP all-league shortstop and pitcher, leading his team to an SCL title and a trip to the NCS championship game. (Photo by Greg Clementi)

(2) VERSATILITY El Molinos Jesse Fichman earned multiple letters in basketball and volleyball during a stellar sports career for the Lions. He was an accomplished guard in hoops, rising to become one of his teams top scorers. In volleyball, he helped the Lions to winning seasons competing in the MCAL, helping his team reach the NCS playoffs. (Photo by Greg Clementi)

(3) SKILL Analys Dylan Mathias was a fierce competitor whether catching passes and making bone jarring tackles on the football field or as a hard-hitting outfielder on the baseball diamond. Mathias turned in a stellar senior season for the Tigers in both sports, making a huge contribution on a pair of SCL championship teams. (Photo by Jean Fruth)

(4) EFFORT El Mos Albert Beltran was a rare three-sport star in football, basketball and baseball in a distinguished athletic career, enjoying equal amounts of success in each sport. He was a steady receiver in football and a consistent player on the basketball court. On the baseball diamond, Beltran was the teams starting shortstop and a reliable hitter. (Photo by David Aires)

(5) SPEED Analys Aaron Maher will go down among the schools all-time great football players in a sparkling, three-year career on the gridiron. A fast and sure-handed receiver, Maher was one of the programs best in career yardage and receptions. He was also a top defensive back and a breakaway threat every time he touched the ball on special teams. (Photo by Jean Fruth)

(6) STRENGTH El Mos Taryn Christiansen will be remembered among the schools best-ever wrestlers, finishing a distinguished four-year varsity career with three individual league titles and a pair of trips to the CIF State Championships. As if that wasnt enough, Christiansen also earned varsity letters as an all-league football player for the Lions. (Photo by Greg Clementi)

(7) CONSISTENCY Analys Agustin Reyes was a major force in an impressive all-league soccer career for the Tigers, one that landed him on several all-league teams and culminated in the SCL Most Valuable Player award last fall. Along the way the Tigers made yearly trips to the NCS playoffs and captured a couple of league titles. (Photo by Greg Clementi)

(8) CHARACTER Connor Hennen will be remembered for his skill and leadership in a long and distinguished varsity soccer career at El Molino. Along the way, he was among the team leaders in scoring and assists, finishing as one of the best in the SCL. He also used his strong leg on the varsity football team as a kickoff and field goal specialist. (Photo by Greg Clementi)

(9) TALENT Analys Nate Kuhn was simply one of the best swimmers to wear a Tigers uniform in a superb four-year career in the pool. A multiple SCL champion and a perennial NCS qualifier, Kuhn shattered numerous school and meet records, earning a place among the all-time greats in the Redwood Empire. (Photo provided)

(10) PRODUCTION El Molinos Baxter Ming was near the top of his class in a terrific varsity football and baseball career, rising to become a team leader in both sports. He was a two-way all-league lineman on the gridiron, and a consistent catcher/first-baseman and pitcher in baseball, one that could hit for both power and average. (Photo by Greg Clementi, page B2)

(11) TENACITY Analys Geddy Dukes was a force in the paint in a tremendous, three-year varsity hoops run for the Flying Tigers, helping his teams to a pair of SCL titles and yearly trips to the NCS playoffs. A shot-blocking machine with a penchant for spectacular breakaway dunks, Dukes struck fear in opponents and was among the Tigers top rebounders during his tenure. (Photo by David Aires)

(12) PERFORMANCE Analys Mohanned Halaweh reigned as a sharp-shooting basketball guard in a sparkling multi-year all-league run for the Flying Tigers, a career that helped his team to multiple SCL titles and perennial trips to the post-season. Along the way, Halaweh was his teams leading scorer and a deadly accurate shooter from the perimeter. (Photo by David Aires, page B2)

(13) DETERMINATION Analy basketball guard Josiah Sorensen could both handle the point and shoot the rock with equal success in a stellar basketball career for the Flying Tigers, one which produced multiple league titles and individual SCL accolades. Sorensen was part of a great nucleus of veterans that could run the floor and defend, stamping the Tigers as a dynasty during his tenure. (Photo by David Aires, page B2)

(14) LONGEVITY Analys Gabe Greenberg-Pines was as consistent as they come in a tremendous three-year varsity hoops career, providing equal parts court leadership and production. A reliable penetrator, defender, and scorer, Greenberg-Pines would find a way to beat an opponent and did so often in helping the Flying Tigers reach great heights. (Photo by David Aires, page B2)

(15) VERSATILITY El Mos Monico Gutierrez was rock solid for the Lions in a strong run for the varsity football and baseball teams. Gutierrez used strength and agility to his distinct advantage on both sides of the ball in football, finishing among the teams leading tacklers. He also saw action on the varsity baseball diamond as a strong contributor in the Lions run to the NCS playoffs. (Photo by Greg Clementi, page B2)

(16) AGILITY El Mos Frank Nolan was a rock of stability on the gridiron as a quarterback and playmaker for the Lions during the fall, but he also enjoyed some fine moments in track and field. The talented Nolan capped a strong senior campaign this spring by winning an individual league title in the Long Jump event at the SCL Championships. (Photo by Greg Clementi, page B2)

(17) SPEED Analys Kyle Lewis was the quintessential football player for the Tigers in an impressive multi-season run, rising to become a key cog in the teams offense. A fast and durable running back and defender, Lewis enjoyed a great senior campaign when he joined the elite 1000-yard rushing club and helped lead his team to a coveted SCL championship. (Photo by Jean Fruth, page B2)

(18) ENDURANCE El Mos Gustavo Vargas will go down as one of his schools best-ever long distance runners in a four-year career in cross-country and track. An all-leaguer in the fall, he was among the best trail runners in the SCL. Vargas may have been at his best in the spring when he made yearly trips to the NCS Redwood Empire Qualifier, advancing to the MOC this spring. (Photo by Michael Lucid, page B2)
